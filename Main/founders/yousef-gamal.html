The story

Yousef has a particular kind of engineering personality — the kind that doesn't wait for permission. He doesn't sketch features in a notebook. He opens his IDE and builds them. That instinct to ship has defined his entire career.

He started in software engineering and never stopped building. Over 8+ years, he worked across Android development, full-stack web, and real-time systems — accumulating the kind of depth that only comes from actually shipping products that real people use. One of his solo projects, Askelp, is a multilingual Q&A mobile app he planned, designed, coded, and published to the Google Play Store entirely alone — with features like anonymous questions, multi-language support, voting mechanics, and social login. Not a proof of concept. A real, live product.

He completed the Founder Institute program, the global pre-seed accelerator that pushes founders through the hardest early decisions before a single line of product code is written. By the time he met Belal, he had already co-founded two startups. He knew what a real founding pair needed to look like — one person who could hold the product and vision, one who could execute the engineering without flinching. He recognized that combination when he saw it.

As CTO of BrainsMingle, Yousef owns everything that runs — the real-time video matching engine that pairs two professionals in seconds, the platform infrastructure that scaled to 85+ countries, and every technical decision about how the product evolves. He builds from Toronto as a digital nomad, which means he operates the way the best remote engineers always do: with high autonomy, high output, and zero dependency on anyone being in the same room.

When a session of 59 founders generates 414 face-to-face connections in 90 minutes with zero moderation, that's Yousef's work.

Featured project: Askelp

Before BrainsMingle, Yousef built Askelp — a multilingual Q&A mobile app for Android — entirely by himself. That means he wrote the brief, designed the experience, wrote every line of code, and shipped it to Google Play. The app includes anonymous question-asking, multi-language support, public feeds, voting, category creation, social login, and category-based follows.

It's not a side project. It's a complete consumer product built by one engineer with no team, no external design, and no shortcuts. The same hands-on conviction — that the best way to validate an idea is to build it to the point where someone actually uses it — drives everything he builds at BrainsMingle.

What he's building now at BrainsMingle

Yousef's current focus is on the matching engine and the session infrastructure that has to work flawlessly every time. As BrainsMingle expands into new verticals — AI & Tech, Fintech, Entrepreneurship, Marketing — and grows its community of experts and professionals, the engineering demands grow with it. Real-time video at scale, with automated matching across hundreds of concurrent participants, is a genuinely hard problem.

He's also working on the platform layer that powers expert sessions and paid consultations — the technical backbone that lets knowledge become revenue without any friction between the expert and their audience. If you're an engineer interested in what he's building, his work is traceable at github.com/youseftcg and his thinking is on X @youseftcg.
