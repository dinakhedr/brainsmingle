The story

Belal grew up in Egypt with a mind that refused to stay in one lane. He could spend a morning debugging a web application and an afternoon reading about wireless antenna physics — and find both genuinely fascinating. That restlessness turned into a career that looks, from the outside, like it belongs to three different people: a hardware researcher, a software engineer, and a startup founder. To Belal, they're all the same person asking the same question — how do you build something that actually works at scale?

He moved to Finland for his Master's degree at the University of Oulu, where his thesis work produced one of the first stretchable Bluetooth antenna designs for wearable electronics — hardware so novel it pushed the boundary of what was possible in low-power wireless communication. He didn't stop there. He stayed for a PhD in Mixed-Signal IC Design, while simultaneously working as an engineer at Texas Instruments. Research by day, chip design by evening, and somewhere in between — building startups.

Before BrainsMingle, he co-founded Alaaddin through Founder Institute Turkey's 2021 cohort — a communication tool for traditional small businesses that reached thousands of users during testing. The Founder Institute director described him as "a passionate entrepreneur with a keen eye for new trends... very agile when it comes to developing and testing product concepts."

But Belal kept running into the same wall that every professional in his network ran into: the right people existed — you just couldn't reach them. Not really. LinkedIn gave you connections. It didn't give you conversations. So he decided to build something that did.

As CEO of BrainsMingle, Belal owns the product vision, go-to-market strategy, and community activation. He's the founder who launches publicly on Hacker News, posts raw traction numbers on Indie Hackers, writes in Arabic and English, and treats the community of professionals using the platform as his first team. He's also, by his own description, a dad — which means he knows better than most that time is finite and every connection needs to count.

What he's building now at BrainsMingle

Belal describes BrainsMingle's current direction as "Substack for live sessions" — the fastest way for experts to host paid live sessions, workshops, and crash courses. He's focused on three fronts simultaneously: growing the expert and community supply side in the Arab world and diaspora, activating demand through targeted GTM across professional verticals, and building the product features that turn a first networking session into a lasting professional relationship.

He posts publicly and regularly — traction numbers, product updates, market observations, and questions to the community — in both English and Arabic. If you want to understand what he's thinking about BrainsMingle's direction right now, his LinkedIn and X profile are updated as regularly as any product changelog.
